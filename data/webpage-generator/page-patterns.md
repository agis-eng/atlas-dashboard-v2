# Page Patterns

Use these as pattern intelligence, not templates to copy literally.

## Core page recipe
Most high-converting pages should establish, in order:
1. clear offer
2. who it is for
3. credibility / proof
4. what happens next
5. primary CTA

## Strong hero patterns
- **Editorial authority:** bold headline, concise supporting copy, one proof strip, one crisp CTA cluster
- **Premium service:** strong trust language, calm spacing, credibility close to hero, refined supporting visuals
- **Modern technical:** product or system framing, compact proof bar, sharper contrast, denser information hierarchy
- **Local-trust service:** human warmth, locality markers, contact options, proof/testimonials close to the first CTA

## Good section archetypes
- proof bar (logos, counts, outcomes)
- capability or service blocks with concrete language
- process / how it works timeline
- before/after or problem/solution comparison
- testimonial or social-proof block
- FAQ to reduce friction
- final CTA with trust reinforcement

## Rhythm guidance
- alternate text-led and visual-led sections
- avoid stacking four identical card grids in a row
- let one section breathe; let another be information-dense
- repeat the CTA only where it matches decision readiness

## Copy guidance
- specific beats vague
- outcomes beat adjectives
- trust beats hype
- believable CTA beats aggressive CTA
